Chicken Cordon Bleu

Description:

A cordon bleu or schnitzel cordon bleu is a dish of meat wrapped around cheese (or with cheese filling), then breaded and pan-fried or deep-fried. Veal or pork cordon bleu is made of veal or pork pounded thin and wrapped around a slice of ham and a slice of cheese, breaded, and then pan-fried or baked. For chicken cordon bleu, chicken breast is used instead of veal. Ham cordon bleu is ham stuffed with mushrooms and cheese.

Ingredients:

  • nonstick cooking spray
  • 4 skinless, boneless chicken breast halves
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 6 slices Swiss cheese
  • 4 slices cooked ham
  • 1/2 cup seasoned bread crumbs

Steps:

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Coat a 7x11-inch baking dish with nonstick cooking spray.
  2. Pound chicken breasts to 1/4 inch thickness.
  3. Sprinkle each piece of chicken on both sides with salt and pepper. Place 1 Swiss cheese slice and 1 ham slice on top of each breast.
  4. Roll up each breast and secure with toothpicks.
  5. Place in the prepared baking dish and sprinkle chicken evenly with bread crumbs.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven until chicken is no longer pink, 30 to 35 minutes.
  7. Remove from the oven, and place 1/2 Swiss cheese slice on top of each breast.
  8. Return to the oven until cheese has melted, 3 to 5 minutes. Remove toothpicks and serve immediately.
